Lesson 1:Requirement Engineering

Requirement is the systematic process of defining,documenting and maintaining the requirements of the software or system.This involves the understanding the stakeholders wishes and needs in order to develop system that match with the expectation of the stakeholders needs means ensuring the system will meet these needs

Types of requirements 
1) Functional Requirement: For functional requirements this are requirement that give or explain the behavior or the function of the system 
2)Non Functional Requirement:for non functional requirements are requirement that describes the quality attributes of the system such performance,security,usability and scalability like it must support like 100000 end users without crashing


Importance of Requirement Engineering 
- Foundation of System development
-Cost of Efficiency
-Reduction in Project Failure
-Stakeholder Alignment

Stakeholders in the Requirement Engineering 
-End users
-Customers/clients
-Project Managers
-Regulators
-System developer/Engineers

Challenges for Reguirement Engineering
-Communication Gaps
-Evolving Requirements
-Stakeholders Conflicts
-Ambiguity

lesson 3: Feasibility study
Feasibility is the systematic evaluation conducted before launching the project to determine whether it is practical,beneficial and viable

Purpose
-Evaluate project viability across financial,technical and operational areas
-Guide Go/No Go decision
-Provide evidence based foundation for investement
-Identify risks and constraints

Strategies (Objectives)
-Assess resources avialability and sustainability
-Evaluate the overall project viability
-Ensure Alignment with instituonal and national Strategies
-Identify and mitigate potential risks
-Support informed decision-making
 
 Core Components of Feasibility study
 -Technical feasibility
 -Economic feasibility
 -Operational feasibility
 -Schedule Feasibility
 -Environment and Political Feasibility
 -legal and ethical feasibility

 Process Overview
 -Preliminary Analysis
 -Data collection
 -Analysis of alternative
 -Feasibility evaluation
 -Recommendation and Conculsion

 Key Deliverables
 -Executive summary
 -Feasibility matrix
 -Risk Register
 -Implementation Recommendation
 -Financial Summary(ROI,Cost Estimates)

 Lesson 4:Requirement Elicitation

Requirement Elicitation is the process of gathering,identifying and understanding the needs and expectations of stakeholders for system and software
 Importance of Requirement Elicitation
 1- Improves good communication between stakeholders and development team 
 2-Reduce project risk and costly reworks
 3-Ensure system meets the stakeholder expectations needs
 4-It helps to defining clear and projects goals

Techniques of Requirement Elicitation
-Interviews:Structured,Unstructured and Semi structured 

Best Practices for effictive Requirement Elicitation
-Engage all relevant stakeholder early 
-Clear define the scope and objectives before Elicitation begins
-Use  multiple elicitation Techniques for data collection
-Document the requirement systematically to avoid misinterpretation
-Validate and verify  requirement regularly with stakeholder

Lesson 5:Requirement Analysis
Requirement Analysis is the the phase of requirement engineering that is essential where the raw data collected form stakeholders is  refined into precise,actionable requuirement.This ensures that the requirement are well understood,unambiguous and meet with stakeholder expectation

Requirement Prioritization is the requirement deal with ranking the requirement to determine which is essential for project success and which are desirable but mandatory
Not all requirement are crictical or equal some are not necessary
Requirement Prioritization helps to determine :what can built first,what can be waited and what can not be built at all
There are techniques of Requirement Prioritization
1.MoSCoW method:Must have,should have,could have won't have
2.Kano Model:Basic needs,Performance needs and Excitement needs
3.100-Point method

Conflict Resolution is the process of identifying and addressing disagreement between stakeholder regarding system requirement
 Techniques of Conflict Resolution
 1.Negotiation
 2.Faciliation
 3.Consensus Building
